### Section 1 — Executive Summary

**Market position.** Integrity Chimney Services is a 14-year-old, BBB A+ accredited, owner-operated chimney/masonry/roofing hybrid sitting in the dead center of Merrimack County's most lucrative real estate gravity well. Bow's August 2025 median single-family price was **$740,000** — the highest in Merrimack County and roughly 35% above the state median of $550,000 — with neighboring Hopkinton at $513,750 and Concord at $507,500 (Concord Monitor / NH Association of Realtors data). Kevin Fredrickson is uniquely positioned: he is the only sole-owner-operator in central NH who openly markets across three trades (chimney + full masonry + roofing) plus historic restoration, and he sits inside a 30-mile ring of pre-1900 colonial center-chimney housing stock (Bow, Canterbury Shaker Village area, Hopkinton, Henniker, Loudon, Pembroke) that the dominant competitors — Crown Chimney, Anything Chimney, Black Moose, Ceaser — do not specifically target. The market position is structurally strong; the digital position is structurally absent. With no website, no Google Business Profile, and a 3.7-star / 19-review Google footprint anchored by visible complaints about response time and follow-through, Integrity is invisible in the exact moment of intent (the search query) and unconvincing in the exact moment of evaluation (the reviews).

**Biggest opportunity / competitive landscape.** The central-southern NH chimney category is dominated by four players with visible online machines — Crown Chimney (Hooksett, ~164 reviews, 4.6★, polished website, deep affiliations), Anything Chimney (Auburn, 4.9★ HomeAdvisor, transparent pricing pages, the only NH competitor publishing actual price ranges on its site), Black Moose (Antrim, NCSG/CSIA-certified, Angi Super Service Award), and Ceaser Chimney (Manchester, 1,036 reviews on Birdeye, 4.9★, veteran-owned, full showroom). Yet despite that machine-grade footprint, the category as a whole is mediocre on three dimensions: (1) **historic/colonial restoration positioning is essentially unclaimed** — Chimney Savers VT is the only player with a clearly merchandised historic restoration portfolio, and they're 90+ minutes from Bow; Steppingstones Masonry is the only NH Preservation Alliance member chimney-adjacent contractor with clear historic credibility; (2) **multi-trade scope is a vacuum** — no chimney company in central NH credibly cross-sells roofing as an integrated offering, and no roofer credibly cross-sells masonry chimney work; (3) **realtor-channel conversion infrastructure is undeveloped** — Anything Chimney and Fire N' Stone advertise Level 2 inspections, but none publish a 24-hour-turnaround written-report guarantee, none publish a realtor-specific landing page, and none publish a same-week scheduling guarantee for transactions under contract. The biggest single opportunity is to **own the trifecta of Historic Restoration + Real Estate Inspections + Roofing-Plus-Chimney Bundle** as the primary positioning, with chimney sweep as the demand-capture wedge.

**Real threats / strategic imperative.** The visible threats are (a) Crown Chimney's installed-base advantage and Nextdoor/Google saturation in Bow, Hopkinton, and Concord — they are explicitly recommended by name in Concord and Bow Nextdoor threads from 2021–2022; (b) Anything Chimney's pricing transparency and aggressive online booking capability ("Book Online Now") which captures the chimney-sweep impulse search; (c) Ceaser Chimney's veteran-owned brand, showroom, stove-sales arm, and 1,000+ review base which makes them the default comparable for any high-ticket repair quote; (d) Integrity's own existing review damage — multiple Google reviews (referenced via Birdeye snapshot) documenting "no response," "promises broken," "left job uncompleted," "the cap was never properly attached." These are not minor — they are the precise complaints that the broader category uses against itself ("the chimney guy disappeared"). The strategic imperative is therefore three-fold and non-negotiable: **(1) launch a website that tells the historic-restoration story with photographs that no competitor in NH has online; (2) operationalize a 4-business-hour callback / next-day-estimate service-level promise and publish it on the homepage; (3) bundle chimney + roofing into a single "exterior envelope" offer that no competitor in central NH currently sells as one product**. Skipping any of these three will leave Integrity competing on price against companies with 100×–250× the review volume.

### Section 2 — Target Audience Deep Dive

**Primary persona — "Sarah Whitcomb, the Bow Center Cape Owner" (age 38)**

Sarah and her husband Mike (40) bought a 2,400-sqft 1798 Cape with attached ell on a wooded 1.4-acre lot in Bow center for $695,000 in 2023. Two kids (ages 7 and 4), one Labrador. Sarah works hybrid as a healthcare program manager at Concord Hospital; Mike is a structural engineer commuting to Manchester. Combined HHI ~$210K. They've been in the house long enough to use the wood stove insert in the family-room fireplace for two winters. Last fall they smelled creosote, the wood stove drafted poorly during a January nor'easter, and water stains appeared on the dining-room ceiling adjacent to the center chimney. They want one company who can: inspect, fix, line if needed, repoint the stack, replace the cap with something that "looks like it belongs on this house" (custom copper or stainless), and ideally also look at the 18-year-old asphalt roof which is nearing replacement. Sarah found Integrity on Nextdoor and a realtor friend's recommendation. She is **terrified of two things specifically**: (a) a chimney fire while the kids sleep — she has read NFPA stats and saw a friend's attic fire in Hopkinton last winter; (b) being scammed or upsold by a "chimney guy" who shows pictures of "cracks" she can't verify. She will spend $8K–$25K without flinching if the trust signals are right. She will not call back a vendor who doesn't return her first call within 24 hours.

**Secondary persona 1 — "Dan the Buyer's Agent" (age 47, Coldwell Banker / RE/MAX, Concord office)**

Dan closes 22–32 sides per year in Merrimack and Hillsborough counties. His pre-purchase due diligence cycle has hard 10–14 day inspection windows. He has been burned three times in two years by general home inspectors who flagged "chimney needs further evaluation" and then he scrambled to find a CSIA-certified sweep who could deliver a Level 2 written report in 48 hours. He wants: same-week scheduling, a clean PDF report with photos and recommendations he can email to the lawyer, a simple price ($275–$450 typical NH range), and a phone number that rings during business hours. He will refer the same vendor 10–20 times per year if the experience is frictionless. Almost no NH chimney company is currently merchandising this channel.

**Secondary persona 2 — "Bob and Linda the Henniker Antique Owners" (ages 64 and 62)**

Live in a 1782 Federal in Henniker with three flues (kitchen with beehive bake oven, parlor Rumford fireplace, central heating). Bob restored the wide-plank floors himself; Linda is on the local heritage commission. They will only hire someone who understands lime mortar versus Portland cement, will not damage original brick, and will take photos before/during/after for their archive. They have called Chimney Savers VT before but the wait is 4–8 weeks. They are looking for a local NH equivalent and will pay 20–30% premium for the right craftsperson. This is the buyer the rest of the market is ignoring.

**Secondary persona 3 — "Tyler the Wood Stove Guy" (age 33, Loudon)**

Heats 2,200 sqft with a Blaze King Princess on 4 cords/year. Knows the difference between a 6" liner and an 8" liner. Posts on Hearth.com. Burns oak that's been split for 18 months. Wants a chimney sweep who will not condescend, who will use a top-down brush and a HEPA vacuum, who knows what 1/8" creosote means, and who will bill $200–$280 with a written inspection sheet. He will become an annual customer and refer his hunting/lumber buddies if the relationship is professional. This is the lowest-margin but highest-frequency persona.

**Top 10 buying triggers (sourced)**

1. **First fall heating-season approach (August–October)** — Hearth.com, Today.com and CSIA all confirm the seasonal spike; Anything Chimney's site explicitly says "we tend to get very busy after fall begins."
2. **Buying a home / purchase under contract** — Anything Chimney, Fire N' Stone, Black Moose, Chimney Savers, Brickliners all have dedicated Level 2 / real estate inspection landing pages; NFPA 211 mandates a Level 2 on transfer.
3. **Selling a home (pre-listing)** — Realtors in NH increasingly request a Level 2 to pre-empt buyer renegotiation per Coldwell Banker / NHAR commentary.
4. **Chimney leak / water stain on interior ceiling or wall** — Crown Chimney customer review: "Our most recent contact involved a water problem that was ongoing for 2 years."
5. **Smell of creosote / smoke entering the room when burning** — HMS Chimney, Chimney Solutions, Fire N' Stone repeatedly cite this as the #1 inbound call reason.
6. **Failed home inspection / "further evaluation" callout** — Structure Check NH: "we had the chimney inspected prior to buying and good thing we did as the estimate of work came back at ~$10,000."
7. **New wood stove or insert installation / fuel change** — NFPA 211 explicitly mandates Level 2 on appliance change.
8. **Visible damage: spalling brick, crumbling crown, falling mortar** — Repeatedly cited in HomeGuide, Angi, On Duty Chimney Massachusetts.
9. **Chimney fire (audible "freight train" or smoke event)** — NFPA 211 mandates Level 3; Hearth.com threads consistently document.
10. **Animal noise in the chimney** (bird, raccoon, squirrel) — Crown Chimney review: "they saved my cat's life - he was stuck upside down in a dead end flue in our antique chimney." Birdeye chronic call-driver.

**Top 10 buying blockers (sourced)**

1. **Fear of being scammed by cold-call chimney "specialists"** — Chimney Savers, Fire N' Stone, Spotless Chimney all publish dedicated scam-warning pages; Wellesley Police documented a $49 cold-call sweep that turned into a $2,000 liner upsell.
2. **No phone callback / company "ghosts" the homeowner** — Integrity's own existing Google reviews: "we got no response. The last contact we've had was in July." Crown Chimney complaint: "Didn't call me back after 2 voicemails."
3. **Aggressive upsell during the inspection ("you need a $26K rebuild")** — Anything Chimney Nextdoor thread: "We were told it would cost $26k to rebuild the chimney halfway up." Hearth.com: "they want 5 grand for the job and won't touch it unless I put 2 grand down up front."
4. **Inability to verify reported damage (homeowner can't see roof)** — Crown Chimney review: "I am kind of disappointed that I paid $300 for two flues to be inspected and all they did was take a point and shoot camera photo to point out streaks which may or may not have been cracks."
5. **Estimate doesn't match final invoice** — Black Moose review: "the estimate provided did not reflect this and the final invoice was significantly more than the estimate provided."
6. **Job left incomplete; cap "never properly attached"** — Integrity's own existing Birdeye review.
7. **Long lead time / busy season backlog** — Black Moose review: "they are very busy which meant I had a good wait for both the inspection/cleaning, then for a quote on repairs and then for the repairs."
8. **Suspicion of "two-quote spread" — one company says repoint, next says rebuild** — Integrity's own positive Google review: "2 estimates suggested tear down and rebuild, 2 recommended repointing... The price ranges for this work varied by ~30K."
9. **Company won't service oil/coal/specific fuel type** — Hearth.com thread: "they don't clean coal burning chimney's either."
10. **Fear of mess inside the home** — Universally cited; Integrity's positive review: "took his shoes off when travelling between his truck and the basement"; Black Moose review: "Alex and his crew even took their shoes off (it was winter) before entering my house without even being asked!"
